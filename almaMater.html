My Alma Mater and what they mean to me

The beauty of student life is the various memories that we pick up along with the academic knowledge. Looking back, students experience pleasant nostalgia. I too am proud and attached to my alma mater.

My College

KSRCE offered me ample opportunities to analyze myself and to correct my pitfalls.

Supportive and caring staff members counselled me in times of difficulty and suggested ideas for my betterment.

My college life taught me to be independent and to stand up for myself. It also taught me the value of true education and self awakening

My School

Having relocated to various schools, I had lots of exposure to companions from various cultures and states. It taught me harmonious existence of diverse cultures.

Loving teachers gently corrected our mistakes and laid a firm foundation to help us become stronger people.

Joyful learning and playful pranks helped me sail through my school years happily.

Being an Alumnus

Though time rolls on, the memories we made stay with us to bring happiness whenever we seek it.

I believe that a part of us always resides in the spirit of the school or college we attended.

I would like to return to my College as a notable and successful Alumnus in the years to come.

College

I loved the various club activities and competitions that were conducted to encourage the students to bring out their talent. Holihue Art Exhibition and English Literary Club Events are the closest to me because of their interesting contests.

School

Being an ardent Harry Potter fan, I loved how my School divided us into four Houses. I was sorted into Green House and we enjoyed House Matches and House Competitions that brought a bonding among students. Rivalry among Houses was not present and all the bickering was good-natured.

Extra Curriculars

I learnt Bharatnatyam, Chess, Keyboard and Badminton. Art and Craft classes helped me discover my creativity and paved the foundation for my artistic temperament. Essay Writing and Oratorical contests brought out the writer and extempore speaker in me.
